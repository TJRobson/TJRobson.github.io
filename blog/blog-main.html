My Reflections

2nd July, 2016


What is your take on the BDC/EDA experience?

Haha, It’s unlike anything else I have ever experienced. It would appear that it is designed to totally overhaul ones fixed norms, and to challenge their conceptions of work, life and their overall well being.

What are your impressions?

It’s still too early for me to say. But some of my initial impressions are, that it could be fun whilst being challenging.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I think at first I’ll be a little hesitant to fully engage, but in no time I’ll be completely immersed and will do well.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

I didn’t have many that I could relate it to so, I wasn’t going in with too many expectations.

Are you excited to participate in the kind of learning environment? Does it make nervous?

Initially I’m very cautious with my decision making. So I feel mostly nervous and unsure as what to expect. I tend to become excited once I start to see things emerge. I became pretty excited whist working on this (my first) website. I worked on it with my sister, which I quite liked doing. Working together made it more rewarding.

In regards to the Shereef’s web chat:

Overall I really liked it and found it reassuring. I really liked Shereef’s analogy about how becoming a developer is like walking to another city. And while it would seem like others in the cohort are well ahead of you, in reality it’s only a couple small steps. I found this really reassuring. On the other hand, the response to the question on dropout rates elicited some anxiety. If someone said to me they were given 95% chance of surviving a open heart surgery, I would say that’s excellent and that’s life or death. But hearing that 5% of people are will not complete the program and it gives me the sh**s a little.

Time Managment and Creating Habits

3rd July, 2016


Which timebox and habits techniques did you try?

I download the Mindfull app onto my phone. Yet to really start to use it, I’ll start today.

What did I find interesting?

Pomodoro was interesting, it is sort of like treating all your tasks as if your boiling eggs haha. Meditation: In the past I read about how a buddhist monk who participated in a neurological study could begin to trigger the eureka moment more often and almost by choice. He achieved this through meditation. I believe this has huge implications for any who thinks of them self as being creative. In the video “how to create a habit” there is an example about A professional that doesn’t arrive to his office till 3pm. The speaker admired his ability to understand what works for him. I believe to make changes knowing what works for you is very key. The Habits of Successful People: They Start Small, was a good read and mirrors some of my breakthroughs since graduating from art school. One is that big ideas can be fun but being fanciful with ideas/projects is more about ego rather than real interest. It also renders them nearly impossible.

What worked for you?

I’ll say meditation. Starting small.

Which will you experiment further with?

Meditation. I’ve always found exercise and eating well key to my productivity. I really looking forward to the yoga sessions during phase 1.
